WHY DONATING BLOOD?

Donating blood saves lives by providing essential support for surgeries, emergencies, cancer treatments, and chronic illnesses. Each donation can help up to three people, as blood components like red cells, plasma, and platelets are used in various medical situations. TYPE OF DONATION

The human body typically contains around five liters of blood, a complex mixture of cellular and non-cellular elements like red blood cells, platelets, and platelets.

Each component has distinct properties, making it valuable for various medical needs. At blood centers, donated blood is carefully divided into these components, allowing a single donation to potentially impact up to four lives, depending on the number of separate components that can be derived from it.

What is it?

Blood collected directly from a donor into a blood bag containing an anticoagulant is known as whole blood. This whole blood is then processed through centrifugation to separate it into red cells, platelets, and plasma. The separated red cells are then preserved with a special solution, transforming them into what is known as packed red blood cells.

Who can donate?

You need to be 18-65 years old, weight 45kg or more and be fit and healthy.

User For?

Treatment for severe anemia in various conditions, as well as blood loss during childbirth, surgery, or trauma situations.

Lasts For?

Red cells can be stored for 42 days at 2-6 degree celsius.

How long does it take to donate?

The donation process, including the pre-donation check-up, takes approximately 15-30 minutes.

How often can I donate?

Male donors are eligible to donate every 90 days, while female donors can donate every 120 days.
